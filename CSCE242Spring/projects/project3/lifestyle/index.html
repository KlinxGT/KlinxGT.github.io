Dos

  • Cleanse regularly: twice daily to remove dirt, oil, and impurities.
  • Use sunscreen: Broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 daily to protect your skin from UV rays.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink an adequate amount of water to keep your skin hydrated from the inside.
  • Moisturize: Use a suitable moisturizer to keep your skin well-hydrated and prevent dryness.
  • Follow a balanced diet: Consume a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and nutrients for overall skin health.
  • Remove makeup before bed: Always remove makeup before going to sleep to prevent clogged pores and skin irritation.
  • Exfoliate: Include gentle exfoliation in your routine to remove dead skin cells and promote cell turnover.

  • Consult a dermatologist: If you have specific skin concerns, consult a dermatologist for personalized advice.

Don'ts

  • Don't skip sunscreen: Avoid skipping sunscreen, even on cloudy days, as UV rays can still penetrate through clouds.
  • Don't over-cleanse: Avoid excessive cleansing, as it can strip the skin of natural oils and lead to dryness.
  • Don't touch your face frequently: Touching your face can transfer bacteria and may contribute to breakouts.
  • Don't use harsh products: Avoid using products with harsh chemicals that can irritate or damage your skin.
  • Don't pop pimples: Resist the urge to pop pimples, as it can lead to scarring and worsen inflammation.
  • Don't use expired products: Check expiration dates on skincare products and avoid using expired items.
  • Don't ignore your neck and chest: Extend your skincare routine to your neck and chest to maintain consistent care.
  • Don't overdo it: Keep your skincare routine simple and avoid using too many products, especially if you have sensitive skin.
