Eurydice and Orpheus

This is a ancient Greek Legend about Orpheus of Thrace and his wife Eurydice. The version I will be referring to is Ovid's version of the myth in Metamorphoses. This version employs a lot of poetic emphasis.

In the story Orpheus is the son of Apollo, and a talented singer. He fell in love with the beautiful Eurydice and they married happily. Eurydice is bitten by a snake and dies; to see his lover Orpheus descends into the underworld and convinces Hades to let his wife come back to life with a sorrowful melody. Hades told him if he can lead his wife back to the surface without looking at her she will live again.

Different Versions

  • Eurydice tripped and Orpheus turn to look at her.
  • Orpheus couldn't hear Eurydice and was scared he had been fooled
  • Orpheus turned around to reassure the scared Eurydice
